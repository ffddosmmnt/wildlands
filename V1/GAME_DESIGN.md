Project Vision
A single-player survival RPG inspired by the core philosophy of Durango: Wild Lands.
This project focuses on:
Deep progression.
Meaningful crafting.
Living ecosystem.
Player-driven survival journey.
The goal is not to copy Durango feature-by-feature.
The goal is to preserve the feeling:
"I survived because I understood the world, prepared properly, and improved my skills."

───

Core Design Pillars
1. Preparation Over Power
Player strength comes from:
Knowledge
Preparation
Equipment quality
Resource understanding
Avoid simple level-based power scaling.
A skilled survivor with proper equipment should outperform an unprepared player.

───

2. Everything Has Value
Avoid useless resources.
Every material should have:
Purpose
Properties
Advantages
Weaknesses
Example:
Light bone:
Good: Fast weapons
Bad: Low durability
Heavy bone:
Good: High damage
Bad: Slow handling

───

3. Learn By Doing Progression
Avoid generic EXP.
Skills improve because the player performs related actions.
Examples:
Cut trees:
• Gathering skill
Craft tools:
• Crafting skill
Fight creatures:
• Hunting skill
The player's lifestyle creates their character identity.

───

V1 Gameplay Loop
The complete first release loop:
Wake up on unknown island
↓
Explore environment
↓
Gather basic resources
↓
Craft first spear
↓
Hunt weak creature
↓
Obtain better materials
↓
Craft improved spear
↓
Challenge stronger creature
↓
Upgrade camp
↓
Prepare for harder exploration
This loop must feel satisfying before adding more content.

Avoid:

Combat Philosophy
Combat should feel tactical.
Not hack-and-slash.
The player observes.
Prepares.
Engages.
Survives.

───

V1 Weapon Focus
Only spear.
Reason:
The spear represents the entire weapon framework.
Future weapons must extend the same system.

───

Spear Gameplay Identity
Strength:
Safe range
Reliable
Good against large creatures
Weakness:
Limited speed
Requires positioning
Bad when surrounded

───

Combat Flow
Observe creature
↓
Prepare equipment
↓
Approach carefully
↓
Attack during opportunity
↓
Manage stamina
↓
Harvest materials

Creature Design Philosophy
Creatures are not moving loot boxes.
Every creature needs:
Behavior
Personality
Purpose
Example:
Small Herbivore:
Purpose: Early hunting
Behavior: Runs away
Predator:
Purpose: Combat challenge
Behavior: Tracks player

───

V1 Creature Requirement
Minimum:
Small Herbivore
Medium Predator
Large Threat Creature
Each creature must teach different gameplay.

───

Island Design V1
One island only.
Focus:
Density over size.
The island should contain:
Safe zone
Resource zone
Danger zone
Player naturally progresses deeper.

───

V1 Success Definition
The game succeeds when:
A player loses track of time gathering, crafting, and improving.
Minimum enjoyable session:
30-60 minutes.
Quality over content amount.
A small deep island is better than a huge empty world.
