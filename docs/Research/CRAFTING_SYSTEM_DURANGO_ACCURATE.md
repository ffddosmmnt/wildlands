# CRAFTING & ECONOMY SYSTEM
## Occupation-Driven Material Economy (Durango: Wild Lands Model)

**Last Updated:** July 10, 2026  
**Version:** 2.0 (Durango-Accurate)  
**Core Mechanic:** Occupation-based interdependence, learn-by-doing progression

---

## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

### Principle: Occupations Create Economy

Unlike traditional MMOs where every player crafts everything, Wildlands forces specialization:
- **Engineer** is the only one who makes bows efficiently.
- **Attendant** is the only one who makes armor well.
- **Farmer** is the only one who produces food efficiently.
- No player can solo-craft everything (grind time is prohibitive).

This means:
- Trading is mandatory (not optional).
- Clans need diverse members (occupy all 8 roles).
- Economics emerge organically (supply/demand).
- Social bonds form through material dependency.

### Material Scarcity Model

Resources are **limited by respawn rate**, not availability:

| Rarity | Respawn | Farming Time | Use |
|--------|---------|--------------|-----|
| **Common** | Instant | 5 min/stack | Foundation (wood, stone) |
| **Uncommon** | 2 hours | 20 min/stack | Intermediate gear |
| **Rare** | 6 hours | 1 hour/stack | High-tier equipment |
| **Epic** | 12 hours | 3 hours/stack | Legendary gear |
| **Legendary** | 24 hours | 8 hours/stack | Apex/unique items |

**Example:** To get 100 copper ore:
- Respawn: 2-hour cycle (new nodes appear).
- Farming: 20 minutes to gather 100 from current nodes.
- Total: Gather current → wait 2 hours → gather more → repeat.
- Grind ceiling: ~300 ore/day (natural limit).

## 2. MATERIAL TAXONOMY

### Tier 0: Foundation Materials (Common, Infinite Respawn)

**Wood**
- Sources: Fell trees (all biomes).
- Yield: 5-10 per tree.
- Uses: Construction, tool handles, fire fuel.
- Gathering Skill Impact: +20% yield per skill level.

**Stone**
- Sources: Quarry mining (all biomes).
- Yield: 3-5 per rock formation.
- Uses: Construction, tool heads, fortifications.
- Gathering Skill Impact: +20% yield per skill level.

**Clay**
- Sources: Clay pits (near water).
- Yield: 5-8 per pit.
- Uses: Pottery, furnace lining, decorative.
- Gathering Skill Impact: +20% yield per skill level.

**Plants (Generic)**
- Sources: Forest foraging (all biomes).
- Yield: 2-5 per harvested plant.
- Uses: Cooking ingredients, medicine base.
- Gathering Skill Impact: +20% yield per skill level.

### Tier 1: Biological Materials (Uncommon, Creature-Dependent)

**Hide/Leather**
- Sources: Defeat herbivores and pack hunters.
- Yield: 1-3 per creature (depends on quality/level).
- Uses: Armor crafting, clothing, bags.
- Attendant Tailoring Impact: Better armor from same materials.

**Bone**
- Sources: Any creature death (universally found).
- Yield: 2-5 per creature.
- Uses: Tool heads, weapons, decorative.
- Soldier Combat Impact: Better weapons from same bone.

**Sinew/Tendon**
- Sources: Large predators (rare drops).
- Yield: 1-2 per creature.
- Uses: Bowstrings, armor reinforcement, binding.
- Engineer Crafting Impact: Stronger bows from same sinew.

**Raw Meat**
- Sources: Hunt any creature (butcher upon kill).
- Yield: 3-10 depending on creature size.
- Uses: Cooking (primary Homemaker material).
- Homemaker Cooking Impact: Better buffs from same meat.

**Scales/Armor Plates**
- Sources: Defeat armored creatures (rare drop).
- Yield: 1-3 per creature.
- Uses: Rare armor, specialized pieces.
- Attendant Tailoring Impact: Legendary armor unlocks.

### Tier 2: Refined Materials (Rare, Deposit-Limited)

**Copper Ore → Copper Ingot**
- Sources: Mining copper deposits (mountain biomes).
- Respawn: 2-hour cycle (nodes reappear).
- Yield: 5-8 ore per node, smelts to 3-4 ingots.
- Uses: Early metalwork (tier 2 gear).
- Frontier Coalition Impact: Fuel discount for smelt.

**Tin Ore → Tin Ingot**
- Sources: Coastal mining (cliffs, tide pools).
- Respawn: 3-hour cycle.
- Yield: 3-5 ore per node, smelts to 2-3 ingots.
- Uses: Alloy metals (bronze).

**Iron Ore → Iron Ingot**
- Sources: Deep mining (mountain caves, unstable islands).
- Respawn: 6-hour cycle.
- Yield: 3-4 ore per node, smelts to 2 ingots.
- Uses: Mid-tier metalwork (tier 3 gear).
- Rarity: 1/3 respawn rate of copper (tighter gating).

**Coal**
- Sources: Mining coal seams (volcanic regions).
- Respawn: 4-hour cycle.
- Yield: 8-12 per seam.
- Uses: Fuel for forges (essential for all metalwork).
- Strategic: Without coal, no smelting possible.

**Flax/Fibers**
- Sources: Harvest specialized plants (swamp, forest).
- Respawn: 1-hour cycle.
- Yield: 5-10 per plant.
- Uses: Thread, rope, textiles (Attendant primary).

### Tier 3: Exotic Materials (Epic, Boss-Dependent)

**Rare Hides (Saber Tiger, Great Bear)**
- Sources: Defeat tier 3 apex creatures.
- Respawn: 24-hour cycle (one spawn per day per island).
- Yield: 3-5 per creature.
- Uses: Legendary armor pieces.

**Dragon Scales**
- Sources: Defeat dragon boss (island event, rare).
- Respawn: 72-hour cycle (monthly respawn).
- Yield: 5-8 scales per dragon kill.
- Uses: Apex armor only.

**Mithril Ore**
- Sources: Deep unstable island caves (dangerous expedition).
- Respawn: 48-hour cycle.
- Yield: 1-2 per node (extremely rare).
- Uses: Masterwork weapons, enchanted gear.

**Gems/Crystals**
- Sources: Mountain caves, rare environment loot.
- Respawn: Variable (hidden spawn mechanics).
- Yield: 1-2 per node.
- Uses: Special properties, trade currency.

### Soldier: Combat & Consumption

**Starting Skill Bonus:** +20 Melee weapons.

**Unique Mechanics:**
- Doesn't craft (relies on Engineer, Attendant).
- Consumes resources (ammunition, repair supplies).
- Demands best gear (drives economy).

**Non-Craftable Specialization:**

| Item | Need | Why | Cost |
|------|------|-----|------|
| Weapons | Engineer-made | Soldiers use them up | High demand = high price |
| Armor | Attendant-made | Durability degrades | Constant replacement |

**Economic Model:**
- Soldiers are consumers, not producers.
- Drive demand for Engineer/Attendant goods.
- Command higher pay in clans (they hunt high-value creatures).
- Economy revolves around keeping soldiers equipped.

## 4. RECIPE BALANCE & PROGRESSION

### Tier Progression System

**Tier 1 (Hours 0-5): Survival Basics**
- Materials: Only common (wood, stone, basic plants).
- Recipes: 10-15 per occupation (focus on survival).
- Crafting time: 30s-2min (fast, encourages experimentation).
- Cost: Low (learnable without punishment).

**Tier 2 (Hours 5-30): Specialization**
- Materials: Uncommon + rare (ore deposits, creature drops).
- Recipes: 15-20 per occupation (specialization unlocks).
- Crafting time: 2-5min (noticeable, plan-requiring).
- Cost: Medium (must farm 30-60 min for one recipe).

**Tier 3 (Hours 30+): Mastery**
- Materials: Epic + legendary (boss drops, rare spawns).
- Recipes: 8-12 per occupation (exclusive, prestige).
- Crafting time: 10-15min (significant commitment).
- Cost: High (must farm 3-8 hours for one recipe).

### Interdependence Matrix

```
STUDENT (Gathers) → Raw Materials
                        ↓
          Splits into 7 paths:
    
    → ENGINEER (Bow maker) → Bow/Ammo
    → ATTENDANT (Tailor) → Armor/Bags
    → FARMER (Crops) → Raw Food
    → HOMEMAKER (Cooks) → Buffed Meals
    → OFFICE WORKER (Builder) → Infrastructure
    → JOB SEEKER (Defense) → Shields/Defense Gear
    → SOLDIER (Consumption) → Uses everything
```

**Every craft requires Student materials** → Students always valuable.  
**Every hunt benefits from Homemaker buffs** → Cooking chain is valuable.  
**Gear breaks and needs replacement** → Attendant/Engineer always needed.

## 5. CRAFTING ECONOMY CONSTRAINTS (Anti-Grind)

### Design Principles

✓ **ENCOURAGE:**
- Occupation specialization (become master of one).
- Trading (buy what you can't make).
- Clan cooperation (fill different roles).
- Long-term character building (skill growth feels rewarding).

✗ **PREVENT:**
- Solo self-sufficiency (no one-player clan).
- Infinite grinding (natural respawn caps prevent it).
- Recipe spam (each recipe has purpose).
- Wealth hoarding (storage is limited).

### Anti-Grind Mechanics

**1. Respawn Timers (Hard Gates)**
- Copper ore: Respawns every 2 hours.
- Max daily ore: ~300 (even 24-hour farming).
- Forces: Can't skip ahead (time gates progression).
- Effect: Everyone progresses at similar pace.

**2. Storage Limits (Inventory Pressure)**
- Base inventory: 20-30 slots.
- Domain storage: 100-200 items max.
- Forces: Prioritize what to keep.
- Effect: Can't hoard infinitely.

**3. Crafting Time (Opportunity Cost)**
- Tier 1: 30s-2min (quick, multiple per session).
- Tier 2: 2-5min (noticeable, plan around).
- Tier 3: 10-15min (significant, plan day around).
- Forces: Choose crafting priorities.
- Effect: Can't craft everything (time bottleneck).

**4. Occupation Requirement (Social Pressure)**
- No player can efficiently do all 8.
- Must trade with others or farm inefficiently.
- Forces: Clan membership or cooperation.
- Effect: Social bonds form through necessity.

## 6. CURRENCY & TRADING SYSTEM

### T-Stone Currency (Mid-Game+)

**Introduction:**
- Frontier Coalition introduces T-Stones (industrial currency).
- Required for Civilized Island domain upkeep.
- Enables cross-faction trading (universal value).

**Earning T-Stones:**
- Faction quests: 50-500 T-Stones per quest.
- Creature loot drops: 10-100 T-Stones from epic creatures.
- Domain taxes (reverse): Earn T-Stones from players renting land.

**Spending T-Stones:**
- Civilized Island tax: 100 T-Stones/day per domain.
- Rare recipes: 1000+ T-Stones to unlock.
- Cosmetics: 50-500 T-Stones (hats, colors, skins).
- Fast-travel (post-V1): Teleport between islands.

### Trading Mechanics

**Direct Trade:**
- Player-to-player negotiation.
- Escrow system (both give → both receive simultaneously).
- No middleman, direct economy.

**Marketplace Auction:**
- List items for sale (price in T-Stones or trade goods).
- Bidding system (highest offer wins).
- Listings persist 24 hours.
- Commission fee: 5% (T-Stones sink).

**Guild Banking:**
- Clan can deposit shared resources.
- Leader sets access permissions.
- Withdrawal log (accountability).
- Enables resource pooling for clan projects.

## 7. CRAFTING ECONOMY MONITORING

### Telemetry to Track (Live Operations)

- Average craft time per tier (is it fun, not tedious?).
- Material gathering time per type (natural progression?).
- Bottleneck materials (what's holding players back?).
- Unused recipes (what's never being crafted?).
- Occupation distribution (are all 8 being played?).
- Trade activity (is economy healthy?).

### Balance Levers (If Issues Arise)

**If Materials Too Rare:**
- Increase respawn frequency.
- Increase node yield.
- Decrease recipe cost.

**If Occupations Imbalanced:**
- Increase demand for underused occupations.
- Create new recipes requiring that material.
- Buff that occupation's unique perks.

**If Crafting Too Slow:**
- Reduce crafting times.
- Add parallel crafting stations.
- Enable batch crafting.

- Special: 20+ (gems, scales, legendary drops).

**Average Grinding Per Tier:**
- Tier 1: 3-5 hours to unlock all recipes.
- Tier 2: 15-30 hours to unlock most recipes.
- Tier 3: 50+ hours (mastery tier, ongoing).

**Interdependence:**
- 100% of crafting chains connect 2-4 occupations.
- No recipe uses only one occupation's materials.
